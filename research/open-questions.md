# Open Questions

## Carbon Accounting

- What depth is sufficient for meaningful storage?
- Should depth thresholds be defined by meters, residence time, or both?
- How should avoided coastal decomposition be counted?
- How much decomposition occurs during descent?
- What fraction of carbon remains isolated from the atmosphere?
- What uncertainty discount should be applied to modeled storage?
- How should lifecycle emissions from vessels, processing, mining, grinding, and monitoring be subtracted?
- How should carbon stored as dissolved inorganic carbon at depth be treated?
- How should methane formation risk be represented?

## Depth and Residence Time

- What residence time is necessary for the project to claim climate relevance?
- Which candidate regions have slow enough return pathways?
- How do currents, mixing, and upwelling affect treated biomass or decomposition products?
- What depth should be required before field testing?
- How should bathymetry, oxygen status, and benthic sensitivity be combined into a treatment-zone suitability score?

## Spray-Based Sargassum Ballasting

- What formulation clings best to wet sargassum?
- What ballast-to-biomass ratio is required?
- Can treatment be done accurately from vessels or aircraft?
- How can treated mats be tracked?
- What fraction of ballast washes off before sinking?
- Does the binder or ballast create turbidity, toxicity, fertilization, or carbonate-chemistry effects?

## Mobile In Situ Sargassum Densification

- Can compression alone make sargassum negatively buoyant?
- Is crush-only sufficient?
- Is chop + compression better than compression alone?
- Which output form sinks best: puck, pellet, briquette, bale, compacted mat, or extruded log?
- Does compressed sargassum re-float during decomposition?
- What compression pressure is required?
- How much energy is required per wet ton?
- What vessel emissions occur per processed ton?
- What throughput would a vessel need to matter?
- Can machinery avoid clogging under realistic sea states?
- Can fauna-exclusion intake be designed for sargassum mats?

## MRV

- What minimum evidence is required before any carbon-removal claim?
- How can processed biomass be tagged or tracked?
- Can treated and untreated comparison mats be observed reliably?
- Can sinking velocity and depth reached be measured cheaply enough?
- How should vessel logs, output density, and release records be audited?
- How can avoided coastal harm be demonstrated?

## Ecology

- What organisms live in target sargassum mats?
- Which sargassum mats are ecologically valuable habitat versus nuisance biomass likely to beach?
- What benthic impacts occur from concentrated deposition?
- How localized are those impacts?
- What oxygen demand occurs at depth?
- Could microbial shifts or methane formation occur?
- How should deep-ocean impacts be weighed against atmospheric carbon and coastal sargassum harms?

## Governance

- Who has authority over deep-water treatment zones?
- What permits are required?
- What public review is necessary?
- How should transboundary impacts be handled?
- Who can stop a field test?
- What independent review is required before scaling?
- How should the project avoid premature carbon-crediting?

## Alternative Uses

- When is sinking better than compost, biochar, anaerobic digestion, construction materials, landfill, or no action?
- What are the lifecycle emissions of each alternative?
- What alternative uses create durable carbon storage?
- Which alternatives are locally feasible in sargassum-affected regions?
