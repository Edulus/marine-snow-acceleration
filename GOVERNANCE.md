# Governance

Marine Snow Acceleration is a research project concerning possible intervention in ocean carbon pathways.

Because the ocean is a shared global commons, this project rejects private, unreviewed, or premature deployment.

## Governance Commitments

Before any open-ocean intervention, the project would require:

- public documentation,
- independent scientific review,
- environmental assessment,
- regulatory approval,
- community consultation where affected communities can be identified,
- transparent monitoring,
- third-party verification,
- shutdown authority,
- post-intervention reporting.

## Scaling Principle

The right to test must be earned.

The right to scale must be earned again.

## Burden of Proof

The burden of proof belongs to the intervention.

The standard of proof rises with scale:

- a model requires plausibility,
- a laboratory experiment requires mechanism,
- a mesocosm trial requires controlled evidence,
- a field pilot requires permitting, monitoring, and reversibility,
- deployment requires repeated proof of net durable benefit and acceptable ecological risk.
