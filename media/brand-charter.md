# Sargassum Signal — Brand Charter

## Working Name

**Sargassum Signal**

## Subtitle

Forecasting the future of sargassum, coastal protection, and ocean carbon storage.

## One-Sentence Description

Sargassum Signal explains the Caribbean sargassum crisis and investigates whether forecast-driven offshore interception, compression, and verified deep-water sinking can reduce coastal harm while advancing responsible ocean carbon research.

## Parent Project

Sargassum Signal is the public media layer for the **sargassum sinking branch** of Marine Snow Acceleration.

It does not represent the full Marine Snow Acceleration portfolio.

## Core Public Thesis

> Intercept beach-bound sargassum before it harms Caribbean coasts, process it offshore, and test whether verified deep-water sinking can turn a coastal crisis into measurable climate storage.

## Moral Center

> Some sargassum is floating habitat. Some becomes a coastal disaster. The work is to tell the difference, act only where justified, and measure everything.

## Core Doctrine

> Not all sargassum should be sunk. Not all sargassum should be treated as waste. The target is forecasted beach-bound nuisance biomass, treated only where the ocean is deep enough, the law permits it, and the result can be measured.

## What the Brand Is

Sargassum Signal is:

- a media brand,
- a public research notebook,
- a map-and-briefing platform,
- a policy explainer,
- a credibility engine,
- a bridge between technical research and public understanding.

## What the Brand Is Not

Sargassum Signal is not:

- a carbon-credit sales platform,
- a deployment announcement,
- a promise to solve all sargassum landfall,
- an anti-sargassum campaign,
- a claim that all sargassum is waste,
- a claim that sinking automatically equals sequestration.

## Editorial Voice

The voice should be:

- clear,
- serious,
- visual,
- scientifically curious,
- skeptical without being cynical,
- public-friendly without being simplistic.

## Forbidden Tone

Avoid:

- hype,
- triumphalism,
- “magic bullet” language,
- greenwashing,
- war-on-seaweed framing,
- carbon-credit-first framing,
- dismissive language about ecological risk.

## Recurring Brand Lines

Use these carefully and repeatedly:

- The Great Atlantic Sargassum Belt is not a blob.
- Beach cleanup is the last and worst moment to solve the problem.
- Sinking is not storage unless it is measured.
- MRV is part of the invention.
- The target is beach-bound nuisance biomass, not floating habitat.
- Deep enough means residence time, not just water depth.
- No forecasted patch, no avoided-beaching claim.
- No ground truth, no biomass-confidence claim.

## Audience Promise

Sargassum Signal promises to help readers understand:

- where the sargassum is,
- why it matters,
- when it becomes harmful,
- what offshore interception would require,
- what carbon claims would need to prove,
- what ecological risks must be taken seriously,
- what governments and coastal communities need to know.

## Credibility Standard

Every article should distinguish:

- observation from inference,
- satellite detection from ground truth,
- beaching risk from confirmed landfall,
- sinking from sequestration,
- research from deployment,
- concept maps from operational maps,
- coastal-protection claims from carbon-removal claims.

## Initial Visual Identity

Suggested visual language:

- deep Atlantic blue,
- sargassum gold / brown / amber,
- Caribbean teal,
- warning red only for exclusion zones,
- clean maps,
- annotated satellite imagery,
- diagrams of vessels, bales, depth layers, and MRV chains.

## Strategic Use

The brand should support:

- public education,
- collaborator discovery,
- funder education,
- Mexico / Quintana Roo outreach,
- journalist briefings,
- scientific transparency,
- open research momentum.
