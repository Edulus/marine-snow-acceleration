# Ballast Formulation Research

## Purpose

This document compares candidate materials for sinking floating sargassum.

## Status

Draft placeholder.

## Desired Properties

A viable ballast formulation should:

1. cling to wet sargassum,
2. add density quickly,
3. resist immediate wash-off,
4. be cheap enough for large-scale application,
5. avoid persistent pollution,
6. avoid unacceptable toxicity,
7. allow carbon accounting and monitoring,
8. sink treated biomass below target depth before substantial decomposition occurs.

## Candidate Formulations

### Clay-Mineral Slurry

Possible materials:

- clean marine clay,
- kaolin,
- bentonite,
- fine limestone,
- basalt fines.

### Biodegradable Binder-Mineral Slurry

Possible binder classes:

- alginate-based binders,
- chitosan-like biopolymers,
- starch-derived binders,
- cellulose-derived binders.

### Two-Part Marine-Snow Glue System

A sequential system:

1. sticky marine-biodegradable binder,
2. dense mineral ballast.

### Calcium Carbonate or Limestone Coating

Potentially abundant and publicly legible, but carbonate chemistry must be counted honestly.

### Basalt or Silicate Mineral Coating

Potentially dense and possibly alkalinity-relevant, but may merge sargassum sinking with ocean alkalinity enhancement.

### Iron-Rich Mineral Coating

Potentially dense but risks accidental fertilization.

## Evaluation Criteria

- adhesion,
- sinking speed,
- wash-off rate,
- toxicity,
- turbidity,
- decomposition effects,
- oxygen demand,
- lifecycle emissions,
- cost,
- sourcing impacts,
- regulatory burden.
