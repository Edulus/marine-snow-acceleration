# Internet Media Brand Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines an Internet media brand around the sargassum sinking branch of Marine Snow Acceleration.

The media brand is intended to build public understanding, source credibility, attract collaborators, support policy conversations, and prepare the ground for research funding.

It should not hype deployment, sell carbon credits, or imply that sinking sargassum is already proven.

## Status

Initial strategy.

## Strategic Context

Sargassum sinking is one branch of the broader Marine Snow Acceleration project.

The broader project investigates ways to accelerate durable ocean carbon storage by improving the downward transfer of organic carbon, while keeping ecological risk, MRV, law, and lifecycle emissions central.

This media brand focuses on one branch:

> forecast-driven offshore interception, compression, and verified deep-water sinking of beach-bound nuisance sargassum.

Other Marine Snow Acceleration pathways are outside the scope of this brand unless explicitly introduced as portfolio context.

## Public-Facing Thesis

> Intercept beach-bound sargassum before it harms Caribbean coasts, process it offshore, and test whether verified deep-water sinking can turn a coastal crisis into measurable climate storage.

This sentence should guide the brand.

It is deliberately more careful than “sink sargassum in the Caribbean.”

## Brand Positioning

The brand is not:

> Let’s sink all the sargassum.

The brand is:

> Some sargassum is floating habitat. Some becomes a coastal disaster. The question is whether we can identify the harmful portion early, intercept it responsibly, and send its carbon into long-residence deep water.

## Recommended Brand Name

Preferred working name:

> **Sargassum Signal**

Subtitle:

> Forecasting the future of sargassum, coastal protection, and ocean carbon storage.

Why this works:

- It sounds like a media brand, not only a startup.
- It implies observation and interpretation.
- It fits maps, forecasts, briefings, science notes, and policy analysis.
- It avoids overpromising deployment.

Alternative names:

- The Sargassum Brief
- Blue Snow Caribbean
- Seaweed to Deep Sea
- The Sargassum Intercept
- Caribbean Sargassum Watch
- The Sinking Question

## Brand Doctrine

The brand’s central doctrine:

> Not all sargassum should be sunk. Not all sargassum should be treated as waste. The target is forecasted beach-bound nuisance biomass, treated only where the ocean is deep enough, the law permits it, and the result can be measured.

This doctrine protects the brand from three errors:

1. treating habitat as waste,
2. treating sinking as proven sequestration,
3. treating coastal urgency as permission to skip ecology or law.

## Tone and Voice

The tone should be:

- serious,
- visual,
- curious,
- skeptical,
- solution-oriented,
- plainspoken,
- transparent about uncertainty.

Avoid:

- techno-optimist hype,
- carbon-credit language,
- “we will solve it” claims,
- treating the ocean as a dumping ground,
- dismissing marine ecology,
- pretending operational maps are deployment maps.

## Primary Audiences

The media brand should serve multiple audiences:

- Caribbean coastal communities,
- Mexican and Quintana Roo officials,
- tourism operators,
- ocean CDR researchers,
- marine ecologists,
- climate-tech people,
- journalists,
- funders,
- policy people,
- curious general readers.

## Recurring Formats

### Weekly Sargassum Map Brief

A short recurring update that interprets current satellite maps and explains uncertainty.

Must always include:

- source date,
- map source,
- region covered,
- what appears to be changing,
- what cannot be inferred from the map alone.

### Monthly Caribbean Sargassum Outlook

A broader seasonal brief.

Could include:

- Caribbean-wide distribution,
- likely affected regions,
- Mexico / Quintana Roo focus,
- policy or research update,
- one technical explainer.

### Science Notes

Short explainers on:

- pneumatocysts,
- sinking velocity,
- remineralization,
- deep-ocean residence time,
- MRV,
- benthic impacts,
- oxygen demand.

### Myth vs Reality Posts

Examples:

- Myth: The Great Atlantic Sargassum Belt is one giant island.
- Myth: If it sinks, the carbon is stored forever.
- Myth: All sargassum is waste.
- Myth: Beach cleanup solves the problem.

### Visual Explainers

Examples:

- why the shelf is a no-sink zone,

### Spanish-Language Explainers

## Founding Essay Thesis

Working title:

> The Sargassum Sinking Question

Core argument:

> The Caribbean sargassum crisis is not only a beach-cleaning problem. It is a forecasting, interception, ecology, and carbon-accounting problem. If we can identify beach-bound nuisance biomass before landfall, process it offshore, and verify deep-water storage, then sargassum may become a test case for responsible marine carbon intervention.

## Trust Rules

Every public piece should obey these rules:

1. Say when a map is conceptual.
2. Distinguish satellite detection from ground truth.
3. Distinguish avoided beaching from carbon removal.
4. Distinguish sinking from durable storage.
5. Distinguish research from deployment.
6. Mention ecological tradeoffs.
7. Avoid carbon-credit claims.
8. Link to source data when possible.
9. State uncertainty plainly.
10. Treat critics as useful, not as enemies.

## Relationship to the GitHub Repository

The media brand should translate the research repository for public audiences.

The repo remains the technical backbone.

The media brand becomes the interpretation layer:

```text
research repo
→ evidence, documents, issues, assumptions, maps
→ Sargassum Signal
→ public explanation, policy framing, visual storytelling, stakeholder outreach
```
