# Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification Architecture

## Purpose

This document defines how Marine Snow Acceleration interventions can be measured and verified.

It applies to all candidate pathways, including spray-based in situ sargassum ballasting and mobile in situ sargassum densification.

## Status

Expanded placeholder.

## MRV Doctrine

A project that cannot be measured cannot be credited.

A project that cannot distinguish its effects from natural variability cannot claim removal.

A project that cannot quantify uncertainty cannot claim precision.

MRV is not external to Marine Snow Acceleration. It is part of the invention.

## Measurement Targets

All pathways should measure:

- baseline conditions,
- treated area,
- biomass treated,
- intervention material applied, if any,
- fraction that sinks,
- sinking speed,
- depth reached,
- carbon retained,
- decomposition products,
- air-sea gas exchange,
- oxygen impact,
- benthic footprint,
- avoided coastal landfall,
- avoided beach decomposition,
- lifecycle emissions.

## Sargassum-Specific MRV Targets

For sargassum pathways, MRV should also measure:

- sargassum mat location and extent,
- estimated wet biomass,
- estimated dry biomass,
- estimated carbon content,
- drift forecast and likely coastal landfall,
- target depth beneath treatment zone,
- treated versus untreated comparison mats,
- post-treatment persistence at the surface,
- sinking confirmation,
- re-float risk,
- decomposition timing,
- avoided coastal harm.

## Spray-Based Ballasting MRV Targets

For spray-based in situ ballasting, MRV should measure:

- ballast formulation,
- ballast dose,
- binder dose,
- treated area,
- wash-off fraction,
- turbidity plume,
- chemical leaching,
- treated-mat sinking fraction,
- untreated control-mat behavior.

## Mobile In Situ Densification MRV Targets

For mobile in situ sargassum densification, MRV should measure:

- vessel track,
- operating zone,
- intake rate,
- wet biomass processed,
- estimated dry biomass processed,
- carbon content estimate,
- fauna and debris exclusion performance,
- processing energy use,
- fuel use and vessel emissions,
- compression pressure or process settings,
- output form: puck, pellet, briquette, bale, mat, or slurry block,
- output density,
- output mass and dimensions,
- release location,
- water depth at release,
- sinking velocity,
- fragmentation during descent,
- re-float rate,
- depth reached,
- decomposition products,
- oxygen demand,
- benthic footprint.

## Possible Tools

- satellite observations,
- aircraft mapping,
- drones,
- vessels,
- onboard intake sensors,
- conveyor mass estimates,
- belt scales or flow-rate estimates,
- compression pressure sensors,
- output-unit tagging,
- drifting floats,
- particle traps,
- chemical tracers,
- oxygen sensors,
- carbon chemistry sampling,
- benthic monitoring systems,
- before/after/control comparison zones.

## Minimum Sargassum MRV Claim Chain

Before a sargassum intervention can claim carbon benefit, it should support the following chain:

1. The treated biomass existed and was measured or credibly estimated.
2. The treated biomass was likely to remain near the surface or cause coastal harm if untreated.
3. The intervention made the biomass sink.
4. The biomass reached a depth associated with meaningful residence time.
5. Decomposition products did not rapidly return to the atmosphere.
6. Lifecycle emissions were counted.
7. Ecological and chemical impacts were measured.
8. The net durable carbon benefit remained positive.
