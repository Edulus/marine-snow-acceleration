# Compression-Only Sargassum Test Protocol

## Purpose

This document defines the first experimental roadmap for testing whether floating pelagic sargassum can be made negatively buoyant through mechanical processing alone.

The central question is:

> Can mechanical densification replace chemical or mineral ballast?

## Status

Initial protocol framework.

This document is intended for bench, tank, and controlled mesocosm planning. It is not an open-ocean field protocol.

## Central Hypothesis

Compression may make sargassum sink by crushing gas bladders, expelling trapped air, compacting loose biomass, and forming coherent dense units.

## Experimental Hierarchy

The experiment should proceed from simplest to more complex treatments:

1. untreated fresh sargassum control,
2. untreated decaying sargassum control,
3. crushed-only treatment,
4. chopped-only treatment,
5. compressed-only treatment,
6. chopped + compressed treatment,
7. compressed pucks,
8. compressed briquettes,
9. compressed pellets,
10. compressed bales or mats,
11. compression plus ballast only if needed,
12. binder only if ballast does not remain integrated.

## Stage 1: Untreated Controls

### Purpose

Establish baseline buoyancy, decomposition, and natural sinking behavior.

### Treatments

- fresh whole sargassum,
- decaying whole sargassum,
- loose mat sections,
- dense mat sections,
- naturally fragmented sargassum.

### Measurements

- wet mass,
- dry mass,
- carbon content estimate,
- initial buoyancy,
- time floating,
- natural sinking behavior,
- decomposition rate,
- gas formation,
- fragmentation.

### Success Criteria

Baseline behavior is characterized well enough to compare treated samples.

### Failure Criteria

Controls are too variable or poorly characterized to support comparisons.

## Stage 2: Crush-Only Treatment

### Purpose

Test whether destroying gas bladders and trapped air is enough to produce negative buoyancy.

### Controlled Variables

- sargassum mass,
- crushing pressure,
- crushing duration,
- sample freshness,
- salinity,
- temperature.

### Measurements

- fraction of gas bladders visibly crushed,
- wet density,
- time to sink,
- sinking velocity,
- re-float risk,
- fragmentation,
- decomposition rate.

### Success Criteria

Crushed biomass sinks consistently without further processing.

### Failure Criteria

Crushed biomass remains floating, fragments excessively, or re-floats quickly.

## Stage 3: Chopped-Only Treatment

### Purpose

Test whether mechanical size reduction changes buoyancy and sinking behavior.

### Controlled Variables

- chop size,
- blade type,
- sample freshness,
- salinity,
- temperature.

### Measurements

- particle-size distribution,
- buoyancy,
- time to sink,
- sinking velocity,
- fragment loss,
- decomposition rate.

### Success Criteria

Chopped material sinks reliably and remains below the surface.

### Failure Criteria

Chopped material remains suspended, disperses, or decomposes rapidly in the upper water column.

## Stage 4: Compressed-Only Treatment

### Purpose

Test whether compression alone can make whole or minimally processed sargassum negatively buoyant.

### Controlled Variables

- compression pressure,
- compression duration,
- wet mass,
- sample freshness,
- output size,
- output shape.

### Measurements

- output density,
- water expelled,
- air expelled,
- time to sink,
- sinking velocity,
- re-float risk,
- structural integrity,
- energy per processed mass.

### Success Criteria

Compressed biomass sinks consistently, remains submerged, and reaches simulated target-depth behavior without added ballast.

### Failure Criteria

Compressed biomass expands, fragments, re-floats, or requires impractically high energy.

## Stage 5: Chopped + Compressed Treatment

### Purpose

Test whether chopping improves compression and output consistency.

### Controlled Variables

- chop size,
- compression pressure,
- compression duration,
- output shape,
- wet mass.

### Measurements

- output density,
- sinking velocity,
- re-float risk,
- fragmentation,
- energy use,
- decomposition rate.

### Success Criteria

Chopped + compressed material outperforms compression-only material without unacceptable fragmentation or decomposition.

### Failure Criteria

Chopping increases fragmentation, decomposition, or energy burden without improving sinking.

## Stage 6: Output Form Comparison

### Purpose

Compare different compressed product forms.

### Forms

- puck,
- briquette,
- pellet,
- bale,
- compacted mat,
- extruded log or rope.

### Measurements

- density,
- dimensions,
- wet mass,
- dry mass,
- time to sink,
- sinking velocity,
- descent integrity,
- fragmentation,
- re-float behavior,
- handling reliability,
- energy requirement.

### Success Criteria

At least one output form provides reliable negative buoyancy, measurable output mass, manageable energy use, and low re-float risk.

### Failure Criteria

No output form sinks reliably without ballast, or all require excessive energy.

## Stage 7: Ballast Escalation Only If Needed

If mechanical processing alone fails, test minimal ballast addition.

Possible additions:

- sand or clean inert mineral,
- clay,
- basalt or silicate fines,
- biodegradable binder,
- sargassum-derived binder.

The goal is to determine the minimum additive required, not to maximize additive use.

## Required Data Fields

Each test record should include:

- sample ID,
- source location,
- sample freshness,
- wet mass,
- dry mass,
- estimated carbon content,
- treatment type,
- process settings,
- output form,
- output density,
- water expelled,
- time to sink,
- sinking velocity,
- re-float observation,
- fragmentation observation,
- decomposition observation,
- energy estimate,
- notes.

## Minimum Evidence Standard

Compression-only sinking is provisionally supported only if:

1. treated samples sink consistently relative to controls,
2. sinking occurs without added ballast,
3. re-float risk is low over the test period,
4. fragmentation is limited,
5. processing energy appears plausibly below carbon benefit,
6. results are reproducible across fresh and decaying samples.

## Next-Step Trigger

Move from bench tests to larger tank or mesocosm tests only if compression-only or chop + compression produces reproducible negative buoyancy and acceptable integrity.

Move to ballast tests only if mechanical-only treatments fail.
