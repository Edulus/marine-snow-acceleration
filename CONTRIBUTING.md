# Contributing to Marine Snow Acceleration

Marine Snow Acceleration is an open research project.

Contributions are welcome from oceanographers, biogeochemists, ecologists, modelers, engineers, policymakers, writers, and interested reviewers.

## Contribution Types

Useful contributions include:

- literature references,
- carbon-accounting critiques,
- ecological risk analysis,
- MRV proposals,
- modeling ideas,
- experimental design suggestions,
- pathway comparisons,
- corrections to assumptions,
- governance analysis,
- plain-language explanations.

## Standards

Contributions should be:

- evidence-based,
- transparent about uncertainty,
- respectful of ecological complexity,
- clear about assumptions,
- careful with claims of carbon removal,
- cautious about scaling before measurement.

## Language Discipline

Please avoid treating the following as equivalent:

- carbon fixation and carbon removal,
- sinking and durable storage,
- biomass growth and sequestration,
- natural and safe,
- engineered and controlled,
- modeled and measured.

## Project Doctrine

> Engineer the fall, not the bloom.

All contributions should remain answerable to the governing metric:

> Net additional durable carbon stored at depth after ecological, chemical, operational, and lifecycle costs are counted.
